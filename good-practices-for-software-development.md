# Good Practices for Software Development – Review

## Question 1  
### Which point(s) were new to you?


-I didn’t know about the tools like TimeLimit, Freedom etc., Which can help to work more efficiently.

-The idea of over-communicating. I used to think communicating only when needed is enough, but now I understand that in remote work it is better to inform the team even about small delays or issues.

-I learned that it’s better to use group chats instead of private messages so more people see updates.

-The proper way to ask questions when stuck. Instead of just saying "it is not working", we should explain the problem clearly, mention what we tried, and share screenshots or error logs. 

-I learned that turning on video during meetings improves rapport and communication with the team.

## Question 2  
### Which area do you think you need to improve on? What are your ideas to make progress in that area?

I think I need to improve mainly in communication and focus.

I will ask questions earlier, explain problems clearly, and update my team if something is taking longer.

Will also keep my phone silent, avoid social media, work in focused blocks, and take short breaks to stay concentrated.
